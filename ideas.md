**Design Movement:** Classical Islamic Manuscript — inspired by the illuminated pages of the Quran and Ottoman architectural ornamentation.

**Core Principles:**
- Deep reverence expressed through restrained elegance, not ornate clutter
- Every element earns its place: text, space, and ornament in balance
- Asymmetric editorial layouts that guide the eye like a calligraphic line
- Sacred geometry as a structural underpinning, not decoration

**Color Philosophy:** Forest green (#1a5c4a) as the primary — the color of paradise in Islamic tradition. Warm gold (#C9A84C) as the accent — evoking illuminated manuscripts. Cream (#FAF7F0) as the background — warm, not stark white. Deep charcoal (#1C1C1E) for body text.

**Layout Paradigm:** Asymmetric editorial — hero content offset to the left with a large decorative geometric panel on the right. Sections alternate between full-bleed imagery and structured text columns. Navigation is top-fixed with a slim top bar for contact info.

**Signature Elements:**
- Thin gold geometric border lines (star polygon motifs) as section dividers
- Arabic calligraphy as a watermark/background texture in hero sections
- Crescent-and-star motif as a recurring micro-icon

**Interaction Philosophy:** Smooth, unhurried transitions — fade-ins on scroll, gentle hover lifts on cards. Nothing jarring or fast. Interactions feel like turning the page of a sacred text.

**Animation:** Sections fade up on scroll with 0.4s ease-out. Navigation links have a gold underline slide-in on hover. CTA buttons have a subtle shimmer on hover.

**Typography System:** Headings in Playfair Display (serif, classical weight), body in Source Serif 4 (readable, warm), Arabic text in Scheherazade New (authentic Naskh calligraphy). Hierarchy: 3rem display / 1.5rem section heads / 1rem body.

## Selected Design: Classical Islamic Manuscript (Response 1)

This design philosophy best honors the spiritual gravity of a masjid while remaining professional and welcoming. The forest green and gold palette is immediately recognizable as Islamic, the serif typography conveys tradition and authority, and the editorial layout ensures the website feels crafted rather than assembled.
